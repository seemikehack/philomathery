Welcome to Philomathery.

Philomathery is a syndicate of knowledge seekers who come together to learn, create, and most importantly, share. Visit the blog here, or email michael@philomathery.com.

What is philomathery?

Philomathery is a portmanteau of the words philomath and something like sorcery or tom-foolery. As it pertains to knowledge and learning, if a polymath possesses much knowledge, then a philomath is someone who delights in the learning thereof.

But what is philomathery?

Philomathery is anything one does for the sake of learning and obtaining knowledge, ideally with the intention of spreading new knowledge to others. There are negative connotations associated with phrases like "jack of all trades", but we embrace such ideas and revel in the joy of knowing.

How do you even pronounce that?

Technically, philomath is pronounced FILL-o-math, but I like to say FIE-lo-math, and the English language is such that you can get away with either as long as you are understood by your intended audience.
